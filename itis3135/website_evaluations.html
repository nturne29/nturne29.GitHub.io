Website Evaluations

Discovery Place

This is the website for Discovery Place, a science museum in Charlotte, NC, offering interactive exhibits and educational experiences. It uses a clean, modern design with a light blue, white, and gray color scheme.

  • Contrast: The site effectively uses contrast with a light background and darker text, complemented by light blue accents that draw attention to key areas.
  • Repetition: Design elements like centered sections and consistent typography are repeated throughout, creating a cohesive look.
  • Alignment: Everything is center-aligned, giving it a clean and organized appearance, though the dual headers can be slightly confusing.
  • Proximity: Related content is well-spaced and grouped, though the scrolling animation sometimes lags behind the user’s scroll speed.

Yale School of Art

This is the website for Yale’s School of Art, intended to showcase student work and program details, but it’s chaotic and hard to navigate due to poor design choices.

  • Contrast: Random colors (green, yellow, black) clash with the grid-patterned chair background, making it hard to read or focus.
  • Repetition: There’s no consistent design—colors, fonts, and layouts feel arbitrary with no unifying theme.
  • Alignment: Everything is left-aligned with no clear structure, leading to a cluttered and unbalanced look.
  • Proximity: Information is poorly grouped, links are broken, and the layout feels chaotic due to unrestricted editing by students.
