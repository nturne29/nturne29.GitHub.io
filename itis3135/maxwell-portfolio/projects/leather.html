Colsenkeane Leather Goods & Provisions

A small business located just outside Plaza Midwood in Charlotte, handcrafting heirloom-quality leather goods. Their mission to delight clients through exceptional craftsmanship resonated deeply with my approach to engineering and design.

As their custom leathersmith, I had the privilege of transforming raw materials into functional art - each stitch representing the intersection of tradition and innovation.

Visit Workshop

The products I created while employed by Colsenkeane Leather LLC are their intellectual property, as agreed upon when I was hired. I used their provided tools, workbenches, and materials. My designs and techniques were developed through customer collaboration and guidance from CEO Scott Hofert.

General Product Showcase

As a Custom Leathersmith, the focus was on crafting bespoke leather goods, with an emphasis on quality and customer satisfaction. This role involved both design and production, allowing for creativity in developing custom projects that met specific client needs.

DAY TO DAY

Not only was I offered the opportunity to exercise creative freedom in my custom creations, but I would consistently have a spreadsheet of general orders, including large corporate requests. These included products from our standard catalogue such as:

  • Purses, handbags, clutches
  • Backpacks, satchels, duffel bags
  • Wallets of various styles
  • Keychains, fobs, belts

All of these products were handmade, from the tanned cowhide to the finished item. Occasionally an item would need to be stitched by machine, which I would handle for the majority of the incoming orders during my time at Colsenkeane. As my expertise grew at Colsenkeane, I was entrusted with handling custom orders that came through the workshop. This opportunity stemmed from a well-developed understanding of leather, which I cultivated through hands-on training and dedicated practice to refine my craft.

Custom Projects Highlight

FULL RESTORATION OF A HORSEHAIR-STUFFED BACKREST

My favorite custom project, and most challenging. After two meetings with the customer I finalized my design & received approval. This was a restoration piece to save an aging backrest originally from a vintage desk chair. Havana Brown was selected for its flexibility.

HERMES 'PICOTIN 18' INSPIRED HANDBAG

A reverse-engineered, handmade 'replica' of the popular Hermes Picotin 18 handbag. Requested by a customer in this shade of Denali Leather, complete with the characteristic bucket shape of the original bag.

FULLY CUSTOM SWISS-INSPIRED SIDEBAG

With only pictures from Pinterest, I created a unique take on a vintage Swiss sidebag. My design features an expandable gusset, adjustable mouth for a slimmer profile, and a two piece lid system to allow the bag to be fully opened easily. Crazy Horse cowhide offered enough strength.

Additional Custom Creations

GOLF CARD SHEATH

A customer request for a stylish, minimal golf card holder with storage for two pencils. Crafted in Havana Brown leather with personalized monogramming, this piece combines functionality with elegant simplicity.

MATCHING FULL-STITCH DOCUMENTATION JOURNALS

Designed a pair of Havana Brown leather journals tailored for professional use, featuring internal business card pockets and continuous decorative stitching for a refined aesthetic.

BESPOKE FITTED SUSPENDERS

Custom-sized suspenders crafted with precision, featuring adjustable buckles to accommodate variations in back width and torso height, ensuring comfort and versatility for diverse body types and clothing styles.

ANTLER DAGGER SHEATH WITH HILT CLASP

A specialized sheath for an antler-handled dagger, designed with a narrow leather buffer strip to protect stitches from the dual cutting edges, ensuring durability and secure fit.

ONE-SIZE-FITS-ALL APRON MODIFICATION

Redesigned a Colsenkeane catalogue apron to include adjustable features, broadening its appeal to diverse body sizes. This innovative design continues to be utilized by the company.
